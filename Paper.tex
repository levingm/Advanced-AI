\subsection{Problem Setting and Data}
% - Target variable definition -> CHECK if we want to use EINLAGEVOLUMEN?
The target variable $\mathrm{Einlagevolumen}_t$is monthly end-of-period levels of overnight deposits held by private households, published by the Deutsche Bundesbank \citep{bundesbank_data}. The dataset covers the period January 2003 to December 2025. Six covariates are used to enrich the data set.\\ 
% - Data source(s), time period, granularity MUST CHECK REFS
The Euro Short-Term Rate ($\text{€STR}$), sourced from the ECB
\citep{ecb_estr}, is available at daily frequency and captures
the average overnight borrowing rate of banks in the euro area.
As each published value reflects the previous business day's
transactions, it constitutes a direct, transaction-based measure
of interbank market conditions. While $\text{€STR}$ was
officially introduced in October 2019, the sample period extends
back to January 2003. For the period March 2017 to September
2019, the Pre-$\text{€STR}$ \citep{ecb_preestr} is used as a
direct predecessor series. For earlier observations, the series
is reconstructed following \citet[Ch.~5.2]{winkler2025}.
The German geopolitical risk index ($\mathrm{GPRC\_DEU}$) published on a monthly basis \citep{gpr_index}, quantifies the geopolitical risk in Germany by using automated text search in the U.S. newspapers \textit{New York Times}, \textit{Chicago Tribune} and \textit{Washington Post}.
The month-on-month inflation ($\mathrm{MoM-Inflation}$) sourced from Destatis \citep{destatis_inflation}, calculates the
percentage change in the Consumer Price Index relative to the
preceding month.
The DAX daily closing prices are obtained via the yfinance Python library  \citep{yfinance}, retrieving data from Yahoo Finance \citep{yahoo_finance_dax} for the ticker \texttt{\^{}GDAXI}.
The 10-year German government bond yield ($\mathrm{10Y~Bond}$),
sourced at business-day frequency from the Deutsche Bundesbank
\citep{bundesbank_bonds}.
